Information about Barbados and the Project

Barbados is the small Easternmost Carribean nation that has strong cultural and economic ties to its environemnt as well as and many marine ecosystems.

As negative anthroprogenic impacts persist in our oceans, coral reefs continue to see a decline in health and size.

This will be trouble for both our environment as well as for many communities around the world that rely on reefs for their livelihood unless changed now.

One of the best ways to protect our reefs is to actively monitor them.

By utilizing Google Earth Pro's publically available satellite imagery, 16 distinct reefs were traced at various intervals over a span of 10 years.

This approach aimed to examine shifts in reef area and structure over time across the Western coast of Barbados.

*Area data was generated in ArcGIS Pro and manually added to a seperate js file for circle visualization, GeoJSONs were created from the KML file generated by GEP and grouped to be displayed for the example reefs.
